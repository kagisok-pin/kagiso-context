## Brand Firewall: Nolte Sandton / Pinnacle Stone

**Date:** 2026-02-14
**Category:** Strategic
**Status:** Active
**Entities Affected:** Arc & Mantle, Nolte Sandton, Pinnacle Stone

### Decision
Maintain strict brand firewall between Nolte Sandton and Pinnacle Stone in all market-facing contexts.

### Context
Nolte Sandton operates in luxury segment where brand perception is critical. If seen as "kitchen arm of a stone distributor," premium positioning and pricing power are at risk. Pinnacle Stone serves fabricators who may compete with Nolte, creating potential channel conflict.

### Options Considered
1. **Full Brand Integration:** Cross-sell openly → Dilutes Nolte's luxury positioning, creates channel conflict
2. **Partial Integration:** Mention group relationship to designers but not consumers → Half-measure, confusing
3. **Strict Firewall (Chosen):** Independent brands in market; share infrastructure behind the scenes → Preserves premium positioning, avoids channel conflict

### Expected Outcomes
- Nolte maintains luxury brand equity and pricing power
- Pinnacle maintains strong fabricator relationships without conflict
- Cross-entity synergies captured operationally, invisible to market

### Review Date
2027-02-14 (annual)

### Update Log
- 2026-02-14: Initial decision logged

## Fabrication Capability: Parked (Not Abandoned)

**Date:** 2026-03-03
**Category:** Strategic
**Status:** Active
**Entities Affected:** Arc & Mantle, Pinnacle Stone

### Decision
Fabrication capability development is parked for a minimum of 2 years (revisit no earlier than Q1 2028). Arc & Mantle's current platform focus is two pillars only: Pinnacle Stone (luxury surfaces distribution) and Nolte Kitchens Sandton (luxury kitchens).

Specific evaluated paths — MV Granite acquisition and greenfield micro-cell (porcelain fabrication stealth brand) — were assessed in detail and set aside. The vertical integration thesis for fabrication remains valid long-term; the timing is wrong for this phase.

### Context
Fabrication was previously considered as the next vertical integration step. Decision to park was driven by complexity management: adding fabrication at this stage would introduce operational, capital, and organisational complexity that the business is not ready to absorb. Priority is to execute well on the two existing pillars before expanding the platform.

### Options Considered
1. **MV Granite acquisition (Asset purchase + 70/30 equity split):** Speed to capacity, existing craftsman, technical capability → Too complex for current phase, management bandwidth risk
2. **Greenfield micro-cell (Stealth porcelain/LFT fabricator, trade-only):** Purpose-built for Nolte GP porcelain jobs, channel firewall maintained → Capital and operational complexity still significant; pre-requisite that Nolte pipeline is at sufficient scale
3. **Park for defined period (Chosen):** Revisit in 2028 once Pinnacle and Nolte are performing at structured scale → Focus preserved, complexity avoided
4. **Abandon permanently:** Rejected — vertical integration thesis remains valid long-term

### Expected Outcomes
- Pinnacle Stone continues to serve fabricators as B2B customers (no change to commercial relationship)
- No acquisition targets pursued, no stealth brand developed in this period
- Management bandwidth concentrated on Pinnacle structure and Nolte establishment
- Review in Q1 2028 with fresh assessment of readiness and market conditions

### Risks & Mitigations
- **Competitor integrates fabrication** → Monitor landscape; if a clear acquisition target emerges at compelling terms, revisit earlier
- **Fabricators see Pinnacle differently** → No change to how Pinnacle operates with fabricators; this decision is internal

### Review Date
Q1 2028 (minimum). Earlier review triggered if: compelling acquisition opportunity arises, Pinnacle and Nolte reach structural maturity ahead of schedule, or competitive dynamics shift materially.

### Update Log
- 2026-03-03: Decision logged
- 2026-03-04: Clarified that specific evaluated paths (MV Granite + greenfield micro-cell) were formally assessed and set aside, not just abstractly parked
