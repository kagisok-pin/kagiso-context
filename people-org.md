## Sales Team Operating Model

### Sales OS Components (in installation, from 2026-03-02)

**Weekly Pipeline Meeting (60 minutes):**
- Numbers review (5 min)
- Top deals (35 min)
- Stuck deals (10 min)
- Standards (10 min)

**Discount Guardrails (3 bands):**
- Band A: Rep approval up to [threshold X%]
- Band B: Acting Sales Lead approval [X–Y%]
- Band C: Founder approval [>Y%] + mandatory reason codes

**Pipeline Hygiene Standards (binary — either present or not):**
- Stage, deal value, close date, next step, next step date, last contact date, margin estimate

**Consequence Management:**
- 2 hygiene misses → note
- 3 misses → formal warning
- Targets missed + poor hygiene → PIP

**Sales OS Installation Timeline:**
- Week 1: Pipeline stage definitions + exit criteria; discount guardrails; weekly cadence locked; deal desk rules
- Week 2: Pipeline cleaned; top 10 deals per rep with next actions; discount exceptions documented
- Week 3: Performance snapshots; 2-week coaching sprints for underperformers
- Week 4: Founder step-back test (Kagiso exits weekly pipeline meeting; test if Amy can run independently)

## Marketing Structure

### Current Model: Outsource-First (Adopted 2026-02-14)
**Principle:** Outsource what makes sense now; selectively internalise when budget allows OR capability becomes strategically important.

**10 Marketing Workstreams (spanning Pinnacle Stone, ALTA Ceramica, Nolte Kitchens Sandton):**
1. Brand + positioning (multi-brand, premium)
2. Planning + campaign management
3. Content production (reels, videos, carousels, stills, stories, copy, design templates)
4. Distribution + community (social publishing, enquiry triage, influencer coordination)
5. Performance marketing (Meta paid campaigns, lead gen, retargeting)
6. Trade + partner marketing (designer/architect relationships, co-marketing)
7. Events + activations (Nolte showroom events, exhibitions)
8. Website + search (content, SEO, product pages)
9. Sales enablement (decks, brochures, care guides, FAQs)
10. Marketing operations (briefs, approvals, asset library, budget tracking)

**Phased roadmap:**
- 0–90 days: Minimum viable team + outsourcing mix
- 3–6 months: Stabilise + reduce coordination pain
- 6–12 months: Internalise 1–2 strategic capabilities (triggers: volume, spend, quality threshold, strategic importance)

**Outsource/internalise evaluation criteria:**
- Strategic sensitivity (brand voice, relationships, pricing, IP)
- Learning velocity (iteration speed needed)
- Quality control risk (premium brand perception)
- Data ownership (ad accounts, analytics, customer data)
- Management overhead (briefing, review, coordination burden)
- Cost-to-output (retainer vs per-project)
- Dependency risk (vendor lock-in)

## Founder Capacity Risk

**Current load on Kagiso:**
- Chief Executive
- Chief Strategy Officer
- Chief Systems Architect
- Chief Brand Officer
- Interim Chief Transformation Officer
- Emotional stabiliser

Running simultaneously: Pinnacle Stone, ALTA Ceramica, Nolte Kitchens Sandton, Arc & Mantle, tech stack transformation, org restructure, brand architecture development.

**Three capacity risk types identified:**
1. **Decision fatigue** – strategic + structural + hiring + technology + brand + financial decisions daily
2. **Context switching fragmentation** – expensive cognitive switching between strategy, ops, tech, brand, financial modelling
3. **Identity load** – deeply invested in competence; each initiative becomes partially personal

**Key mitigation:** Sydney hire (Operations Manager), marketing outsource model, and active discipline around "what I will NOT own."
