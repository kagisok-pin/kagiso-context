## Hybrid Supplier Model Context

Globally, slab importers/wholesalers are evolving from pure B2B trade-only to serve both trade and retail/end-user clients. The "missing middle" is lucrative — homeowners want to touch slab AND deal with one accountable party.

**Key insight for strategy:**
- Pure slab sales: 15–20% margin
- Full-service (slab + fabrication + install coordination): 35%+ margin — but requires showroom, marketing, and fitting overhead
- Channel-conflict management is essential: requires transparency (published retail-only fees, protected trade discounts, or product-line/geographic segmentation)

**SA Models to Reference:**
- **Artmar Natural Stone (CT + JHB):** In-house fabrication + installation; one-stop premium positioning
- **Interslab (national):** Wholesale + guided slab-yard tours for homeowners; hands to accredited fabricator (no own install) — preserves fabricator goodwill while capturing consumer mind-share
- **Granite Objects (franchise):** Imports, fabricates, installs via franchise network nationally

**Named Competitors (direct):**
- **Eeze Quartz** — direct quartz surface competitor in Gauteng; price-competitive positioning

## Sales Operating System (in Installation)

A minimum viable Sales OS is being installed (led from 2026-03-02):

- **Weekly Pipeline Meeting (60 min):** Numbers (5 min) → Top deals (35 min) → Stuck deals (10 min) → Standards (10 min)
- **Discount Guardrails (3 bands):** Band A (rep approval up to X%) | Band B (Acting Sales Lead approval X–Y%) | Band C (founder approval >Y%) + mandatory reason codes
- **Pipeline Hygiene Standards (binary fields):** Stage, value, close date, next step, next step date, last contact date, margin estimate
- **Consequence Management:** 2 hygiene misses → note; 3 misses → warning; targets + poor hygiene → PIP
