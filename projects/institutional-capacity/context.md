## What This Is

A consolidated programme covering internal capability building: employee AI competence, a staff knowledge base (agentic RAG architecture), product training, and the tile induction programme. Previously tracked as 4 separate ideas/tasks in Open Brain — now one coherent initiative.

## Strategic Context

Pinnacle Stone is transitioning from founder-dependent operations to institutional scale. The team needs structured knowledge systems and AI augmentation to bridge the gap between current capacity and growth ambitions. Kagiso has ADHD and limited management bandwidth — any capability programme must be low-maintenance to operate and high-leverage in output.

Key constraint: This is "North Star" work for Kagiso — AI competence across the business is strategically vital, not optional. Even when capacity is tight, at minimum a weekly planning session must happen to keep momentum.

## The 4 Workstreams

### Workstream 1: AI Competence Programme (ACTIVE — Weekly Planning Cadence)
**Goal:** Roll out AI assistants across Pinnacle Stone to augment employee capacity and output quality.

**Status:** Planning stage. 4-phase plan exists (captured 7 March). Not yet started, but reframed from "parked" to active weekly planning cadence as of 28 March 2026.

**The 4 phases:**
1. **Foundation (Wks 1–4):** Map roles and tasks, identify high-leverage AI use cases per role (quoting, comms, reports, admin), assess digital literacy, select primary AI tool (Claude or ChatGPT Business/Team plan). Output: role-use-case matrix + tool decision.
2. **Pilot (Wks 5–10):** Select 3–5 employees across roles, assign 1–2 concrete use cases each, run 2-week structured trial with check-ins, capture time saved and quality gains. Output: pilot report with real numbers.
3. **Rollout (Wks 11–20):** Build internal playbook (what AI is for, basic prompting), run role-specific training, appoint internal AI champion from pilot group, set governance rules (no client PII, no sensitive financials). Output: AI as default tool, not experiment.
4. **Embed (Month 6+):** Monthly AI wins sharing, tie to output quality not surveillance, expand use cases (automation via Make.com, AI-assisted quoting).

**Key risks:** Fear of job replacement (frame as augmentation), low adoption (use champions), data leakage (clear rules), tool overload (one tool, one use case at a time).

**Next actions:**
- Run first planning session this coming week (w/c 30 March)
- Complete role-task mapping spreadsheet
- Choose pilot group candidates
- Select AI tool before pilot starts

**Weekly cadence:** Every week, dedicate one session to AI Competence planning. Even if it's just 30 minutes of mapping or research. Progress > perfection.

### Workstream 2: Staff Knowledge Base (QUEUED — After OpenClaw Sprint)
**Goal:** Build an agentic RAG-powered knowledge base for Pinnacle Stone.

**Architecture:** Agentic RAG — AI agents can query and contribute to the KB, not just humans. The KB becomes the foundation for product training, onboarding, and operational consistency.

**Sequencing:** Starts after the OpenClaw/NemoClaw sprint completes (~10 April). The sprint's learnings (agent architecture, skills, security model) directly inform how the KB agent layer will work.

**Scope:**
- Product knowledge: all brands (Pinnacle Quartz, ALTA Ceramica, Mirage, Gardenia Orchidea)
- Technical specifications and installation guides
- Pricing frameworks and margin guidelines
- Competitor information
- Operational procedures and SOPs
- FAQ and common customer questions

**Open Brain reference:** "Pinnacle Stone KB — agentic RAG architecture, sequenced after OpenClaw sprint" (captured 25 March)

### Workstream 3: Product Training Programme (QUEUED — After KB Foundation)
**Goal:** Structured product training for all staff, underpinned by the KB.

**Scope:**
- Tile induction programme (for new staff and existing staff with gaps)
- Brand-specific deep dives (Pinnacle Quartz vs ALTA Ceramica vs Mirage vs Gardenia Orchidea)
- Technical training (material properties, applications, installation basics)
- Competitive positioning training (how our products compare)

**Decision (25 March):** Tile induction is NOT part of the RM role transition workstream. It's part of this broader product training programme linked to the KB.

### Workstream 4: ISO 9001 & Standards (KILLED — 28 March)
**Original idea:** Use AI to pursue ISO 9001 certification without hiring a quality manager.
**Kill reason:** No execution path in current capacity. No budget, no bandwidth, no operator. The idea is valid but premature — revisit in 2027 when the KB and process documentation are established. At that point, the KB itself becomes the foundation for a standards push.
